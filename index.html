Predicting Renal Function Post Nephrectomy for Renal Masses

In this study, we developed a predictive model that uses pre-operative patient characteristics and surgical intervention type to predict short-term postoperative renal function. Data were sourced from electronic health records at NYU Langone Health, involving mainly white males aged 50 to 70, many of whom were overweight with hypertension and diabetes. After data cleaning and preprocessing, we applied Multiple Linear Regression and XGBoost, with and without recursive feature elimination, achieving a mean absolute percentage error of approximately 18-23%. With further refinements, this model could help clinicians and patients choose the best treatment strategy by predicting postoperative renal function weeks after surgery.
